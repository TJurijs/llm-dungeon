Kroll, a free mul — one of the human-dwarf crossbreeds bred for labor and the killing-floor. He stands a head taller than most humans, slab-muscled and hairless, his hide marked with old arena brands and the pale seams of scars. In the pits of Urik he was an enforcer as much as a fighter: the one sent to break the unruly, hold the line, and end things quickly for the crowd. He won his freedom and now sells his strength as a caravan guard, crossing the Tablelands with the merchant trains.

What he is good at, in the fiction:
- **Raw physical power** — few can match a mul's strength; he shifts, breaks, and overpowers where others merely strain.
- **Tirelessness** — like all muls he labors, marches, and fights far past the point most would collapse, and he weathers heat, thirst, and exhaustion better than any human.
- **Violence** — long practiced in close, brutal work: grappling, breaking, intimidation, and controlling a frightened or hostile crowd.
- **The wastes** — a seasoned desert traveler who knows how to ration water, read the land, handle caravan beasts, and survive the open Tablelands.

He is formidable but not invincible: thirst, numbers, magic, and the wastes can still kill him like anyone.

He carries a caravan guard's kit — a heavy bone club and an obsidian-edged blade (metal being far beyond a guard's means), a battered hide harness and desert wrap, a nearly empty waterskin, and a short string of ceramic bits.

Kroll carries a psionic wild talent — a quiet strand of the Way he was never trained in and cannot simply switch on. When his bare skin rests against an object or a place, he sometimes catches its echoes: fragmentary flashes of the recent, strongly felt past soaked into it — a face, a moment of violence, the fear or grief of what happened there. The readings come in pieces and rarely on command, and the sharpest of them — blood, terror, death — can rise unbidden through a hand laid on the wrong stone and leave him shaken. It is purely a sense of the past: it only ever reveals what once happened, and never moves, breaks, harms, or clouds anything in the present — no telekinesis, no psychic force, no power over other minds. He knows the gift for what it is and has learned to live with it, but he keeps it hidden, since a mul who shows the Way is a prize to be chained or a danger to be put down.
